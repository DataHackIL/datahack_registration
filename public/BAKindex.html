What Is HUJI Hackathon?

HUJI hackathon is a students hackathon held in The Hebrew University of Jerusalem. A Hackathon is an event in which computer programmers and graphic designers collaborate intensively on software products. This year’s competition's theme is Smart Cities. The students will be judged on finding and implementing creative technological solutions for various problems in the city, advancing Jerusalem towards being a Smart City.

FAQ

  • Where and when will the Hackathon take place?

    HUJI Hackathon will take place in the Rothberg CS building in the Edmond J. Safra campus of The Hebrew University in Jerusalem. The Hackathon will start on 19/05 in 09:00 in the morning, and end on 20/05 at 14:00.

  • Who can participate?

    HUJI Hackathon is open to design, CS and engineering students from every level of experience. A preparation session will be held in order to teach participants about relevant technologies and platforms, We will host some lectures beforehand to help you prepare, and have experienced mentors on site assisting and advising.

  • Do I need a team to sign up?

    We encourage people to work in teams of up to four developers and one designer. You can register with a team or we can help you find one.

  • Will there be food?

    Yes! The only thing you need to worry about is your project, we will take care of the rest. Food, snacks, and plenty of drinks will be provided throughout the competition. The food will be kosher and the are options for vegetarians and vegans as well.

  • Can I start working before the Hackathon starts?

    No. Contestants are not allowed to work on their projects before the Hackathon starts. If you want to prepare for the Hackathon, focus on gaining experience with the tools you hope to use to build your application.

  • Didn't answer your question?

    Contact Us! We'll be happy to help.
